Dataset Overview

Uncovering Patterns in Aviation Incidents to Enhance Future Safety

Project Overview

This dataset provides detailed information about airplane crashes from 1908 to the present. It includes key variables such as crash date, location, cause, fatalities, and more. This analysis aims to uncover important trends and insights to improve aviation safety.

Features


  • Date: The date when the crash occurred.
  • Time:The time of the crash.
  • Location:The geographical location of the incident.
  • Source: Kaggle Airplane Crash Dataset
  • Operator:The operator or airline of the aircraft
  • Aboard:The total number of people aboard the aircraft.
  • Fatalities:The number of fatalities in the crash.
  • Ground:The number of ground casualties.
  • Type:The type of aircraft involved.

Project Goals:


  1. Identify Risk Patterns:Find common threads in past accidents to spot risks before they become problems.
  2. Make Safety Recommendations:Turn our findings into clear safety steps that airlines and crews can actually use.
  3. Analyze Weather Impact:Understand how different weather conditions affect flight safety across regions.
  4. Study Human Factors:Look at how pilot experience, crew coordination, and decision-making influence incidents.
  5. Create Safety Scores:Develop a simple rating system to measure airline safety performance.
