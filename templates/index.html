Flight Crash Analysis
Introduction This project analyzes flight crash data to identify factors contributing to aviation accidents and inform safety improvements. By uncovering patterns in human, technical, and regulatory influences, we aim to enhance overall flight safety.
Frequently Asked Question

1. What is the objective of this analysis?

-The objective of this project is to analyze airplane crashes and fatalities data from 1908 to the present to identify patterns, trends, and insights related to aviation safety. The analysis aims to uncover factors contributing to accidents and potential areas of improvement in aviation safety standards.

2. What type of data is being analyzed?

-The dataset includes information on airplane crashes such as the date of the crash, location, airline, number of fatalities, number of survivors, and other relevant details such as the cause of the crash if known.

3. What trends or patterns are being investigated?

-Key areas of focus include:Number of crashes over time: How have crash frequencies changed over the years? -Causes of crashes: Are there common factors or causes that lead to crashes? -Geographical distribution: What regions or countries have witnessed the most crashes? -Survival rates: Are there trends in the survival rates across different periods or types of crashes? -Airline safety: Are certain airlines involved in more crashes than others?

4. What tools and technologies are being used?

-The primary tools used for the analysis are Python programming language, pandas for data manipulation, and Matplotlib/Seaborn for visualization. The dataset is sourced from Kaggle.


5. What are the expected outcomes of the project?

-The project aims to provide insights into aviation safety improvements over time, highlight risk factors, and identify any emerging patterns in airplane crashes. This can be used by regulators, airlines, and safety organizations to enhance future safety measures.
