Key Insights

Our analysis shows that while early years saw a steady increase in crashes, improvements in aviation safety over the last few decades have helped stabilize the number of incidents.

  • Major spikes in crashes are noted during World War II and in the late 20th century.
  • Summers show a slightly higher number of crashes compared to other seasons.
  • Post-2000s, a consistent drop in incidents indicates technological advancements in aviation safety.

Conclusion

The decline in crashes in recent decades highlights the importance of continued improvements in aviation technology and regulations. However, certain months and regions still show high risks, pointing to the need for further investigation.
